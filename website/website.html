TaxE - A Game about Trains

"TaxE is the best game I've played in the past 20 minutes"
Developer

TaxE is the latest, and first, game from Team FVS. A story of great suspense, tragedy and jubilance as you must step up and become a train company. Your rivals will try to beat you and leave you with nowhere left to run but you must ensure your transportation empire prevails. Using all the trains at your disposal you need to travel across Europe to get your train to the most destinations in the time you have. Your main competitor won't stand still, so play now to make sure you're on top.

  • Steam

Go back to the roots of the rail indistry as you move steam trains around the game world in a bid to reach your destinations ahead of the competition.

They may be slow but the humble steam train will reliably get you there on time.

Diesel is Petrols more environmentally friendly brother and currently powers a large part of the trains in use across the world today. Take to the rails and race to your destination.

Diesel keeps the world moving, so make the journey to the other side of the continent in a Diesel.

If you've ever travelled across the country before, it was an electric train that got you there on time. Powered by wires above the rails, electricity always keeps running.

Take your journey now, and take it in style with an electric train.

Famous for powering the railways of Japan, the Bullet train was once the fastest there was so you know you're not going to be late today.

The more of these you have, the greater your chances are of beating the competition and winning the race to be the best.

Nuclear energy provides clean and efficient energy to millions everyday and is deployed on different modes of transport already. We add trains to the atomic arsenal.

Nothing can stand in your way with radioactive power behind your attempts to beat the competition and win.

You can travel across Europe to get your trains to their destination as quickly as possible. With stations in over 25 countries there are hundreds of possibilities as to what you can do. Your main competitor operates across the same network so you'll need to make sure your trains are the fastest and taking the best route possible to ensure that they reach the destination first. The player who completes the most goals wins, so you need to make sure that you're keeping on top.

Game Map

There are stations and junctions across over 25 european countries, giving you hundreds of possible routes you can take. The realistic map of the continent makes it easy to find the station you need, whilst the abstraction of the train lines makes it easy to understand just how long each journey will take when paired with the abilities of each train you use.

Stations have infinite capacity so you don't need to worry about where the other player has as their final destination, unless you're on a collision course. All trains sit at a platform so it's easy to pick your train out from the station and give the driver instructions of where his next adventure will be to.

Play the game to explore all the destinations!

Trains

There are 10 different train types in game and upto 14 trains traveling around at any one time, so strategy with your trains is key to your success. Different trains travel at different speeds so some will reach destinations faster than others, so if it doesn't matter what train you send then choosing the right train to make it fastest is key.

Trains don't last forever though. Once your train has made it to it's final destination it leaves you to go off on a new and exciting adventure. You can also choose to let the trains you have go that you no longer need, so strategy is key to maintaing not only the fastest fleet around, but the fleet you need to meet the demands set by your client.

Meet all the trains in game now!

Goals

There are many different types of goals, and just like in the real world transprtation game, you rarely get the same task twice. Sometimes you just need to get from one station to another, but at others you need to make sure the right type of train arrives, otherwise you'll have to do it all over again.

Your strategy is key to being prepared for these goals. Will you keep the fastest trains ready to respond to the majority of goals, or will you keep some less good trains in reserve so you can beat your competitor when the harder goals arrive? The choice is up to you, but you need to be ready for anything in TaxE.

Routing

Not every station is connected to every other station, there is a complex network of twists, turns, stations and junctions to negotiate. You need to get your train there the fastest way, but which is it? It's easy to build up a route and make it to your target destination so long as you have the right train to hand.

Be careful though! You can never have 2 trains at a junction. You don't want to run the risk of coming a cropper and seeing your and your competitors trains destroyed in a horrific crash, or do you? Strategy is key to predicting your opponents moves and remembering what they're already doing. It's down to you, though, if you use this power for good or evil.

Microsoft Windows

System Requirements

TaxE will run on Microsft Windows Vista/7/8/8.1, with at least 1GB of RAM and a recent Intel or AMD processor.

Java must be installed and up-to-date. TaxE may not run with a version of Oracle Java less than 8u25.

Installation Instructions

  1. Install Java
  2. Download TaxE using the button to the left
  3. Run the "taxe.jar" file that downloads

If TaxE will not run then please ensure Java has been installed correctly. Java sometimes requires permission to run, so disabling UAC may resolve issues.

Apple Mac OSX

System Requirements

TaxE will run on Apple Mac OSX Lion/Mountain Lion/Mavericks/Yosemite, on any Apple manufactured device with an Intel processor. TaxE does not support PowerPC.

Java must be installed and up-to-date. TaxE may not run with a version of Oracle Java less than 8u25.

Installation Instructions

  1. Install Java
  2. Download TaxE using the button to the left
  3. Run the "taxe.jaar" file that downloads

If TaxE will not run please ensure Java has been installed correctly. Java may need to be run as an Administrator, depending on your security settings. TaxE will not run on PowerPC based Macs.

Linux (All Distributions)

System Requirements

TaxE will run on all versions of Linux with at least 1GB of RAM and an Intel or AMD processor. A GUI environment (Gnome/XWindows/etc.) must be installed and running.

Java must be installed and up-to-date. TaxE may not run with a version of Oracle Java less than 8u25.

Installation Instructions

  1. Install Java
  2. Download TaxE
  3. Open a GUI environment
  4. Run the "taxe.jar" file that downloads

If TaxE will not run please ensure Java has been installed correctly. You may need to run TaxE as a SuperUser, depending on your security settings. TaxE does not support terminal interaction.

Explore the Code

TaxE is open source, which means you're free to take the game resources and code and make your own version. All of our code is available to download on GitHub so take a look.

Full information for developers is available via the GitHub Wiki for the repository. The key features for developers are:

  • Package and Class Hierarchy make it easy to find the code you need
  • Game data is stored in JSON format, so you can easily modify all data using a variety of tools (Map Editor included)
  • Compliant with all LibGDX standards so code follows through to on-screen
  • Gradle configures all dependencies automatically
  • JUnit Tests included to allow you to test out any modifications you make

TaxE was designed using both Eclipse and IntelliJ, and necesarry configuration is included for both.

Team PIK Modifications

'TaxE' is a pretty dull name for a game, and in its previous state the game had no theme. We took it upon ourselves to reinvent the game with a brand new name and theme
Introducing,

Snakes, on a TaxE!
A game about competing with a friend to transport animals to cities across Europe, in order to achieve the best score!

Most of the modifications are outlined in this trailer:


But here is a list of some of the main features

  • GUI Overhaul

    We completely remade the User Interface to better display goals, resources and notifications during the game
    This includes lots of colours for buttons, a colour scale for the speeds of different train types, routes and notifications, images for each of a player's current goals and each players current score


  • Via Stations

    We wanted to add a further degree of difficulty and variation to the goals that were already being generated

  • Hazards

    The game currently has two distinct hazards: Border Control and Junction Failure

    Border Control

    A random number of stations are chosen to have border control in place at the start of every game If a train passes through one of these stations on its route it has a chance to not pass through, and therefore be destroyed

    Junction Failure

    If two trains arrive at a junction at the same time the trains will collide and be destroyed, and cause the junction to break. The junction is then unable to be used by players until it gets fixed









  • Goals

    To fit in with the theme of the game, the goals now require the user to transport an animal on their train, indicated in the goal title and by a small clip-art next to the goal
    The animals are purely cosmetic at this point and do not affect game play

  • Scoring

    A goal's score is based off how far a train has to travel from its origin station to its destination station, and whether it has to make a stop at a middle station on the way. The score is generated by Dijkstra's shortest path algorithm

  • Speed Boosts

    It is possible to gain a speed boost for a train by visiting a certain station with a train. The boost lasts until the train completes a goal or is destroyed










  • Sound

    A small loop plays in the background while in game, along with voice overs for events done by the one and only Paul Cairns
